Harmful Effects
What Are the Environmental Consequences of Computer Device Production? Creates green-house gases, over extracting natural resources and produces a lot of trash and a lot of water to produce.
Water Usage
  • On average it cost around 1.5 tons of water to create a computer
Natural Resources
  • Aquifer depletion
  • Deforestation
  • Mining for fossil fuels
  • Contamination of resources
  • Soil erosion
How Does Computer Use Harm the Environment? The computers uses a lot of energy, which burns up fossil fuels.
Energy Consumption
  • If a computer was on for 8 hours it's would consume 600 kWh and that would be 175 kg of CO2 per year
What are the Negative Effects of Computer Device Disposal? When metals like lead, mercury, and cadmium are placed in a landfill they can leak into the soil, which can kill crops and contaminate nearby water sources.
Water Pollution
  • Metals can be eaten up by fish and can add up along the food chain
  • We can't eat the fish because of the bioaccumulation
  • Chemicals from e-waste disrupt the aquatic ecosystems
Disturbance to Farmers
  • Changes the soil's chemical compound
  • The soil is less fertile
  • Less crop yield
  • Heavy metals build up in animals and insects that help with crop growth
